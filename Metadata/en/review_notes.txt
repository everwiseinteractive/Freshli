Freshli 1.0 — App Review Notes
Thank you for reviewing Freshli. This app contains Metal 4 rendering and Apple
Intelligence features that are central to the user experience. The three steps
below will ensure you encounter the key innovation moments in under 5 minutes.

No login required for steps 1 and 2. A demo account is provided for step 3.

STEP 1 — Experience Metal 4 Liquid Glass (30 seconds)
Device requirement: iPhone 15 Pro or later, iOS 26, ProMotion enabled.

1. Launch Freshli. The Home / Pantry screen appears.
2. WITHOUT tapping anything, tilt the device slowly left and right (±20°).
   ► You will see the specular highlight on each card track your physical
     movement in real time — this is the gyroscope-driven Metal 4 specular
     shader. The background glass material refracts simultaneously.
3. Place the device flat on a table and watch for 4 seconds.
   ► The interface continues to breathe — a slow idle animation cycle driven
     by the Metal shader, not a pre-rendered video. The surface is always live.

What to look for: the frosted-glass card bodies, the luminous freshness badges,
and the Tab Bar — all rendered by a single refractive Metal shader. There are
no static image backgrounds anywhere in the app.

STEP 2 — Trigger the Gaze-Adaptive Bloom and Tab Melt (60 seconds)
Device requirement: same as Step 1. Haptic intensity: set to maximum in
iOS Settings > Sounds & Haptics before this step for full effect.

GAZE BLOOM:
1. Tap the Recipes tab (second icon in the Tab Bar).
   ► The Melt transition fires: the Pantry view dissolves through a
     noise-field of liquefying pixels edged in green light. Simultaneously,
     your device produces a 400ms rumble that decays as the pixels dissolve,
     followed by a sharp crystalline click as the Recipes screen solidifies.
     This is the Core Haptics "melt dissolve" pattern — tuned to mimic the
     viscosity of the material changing state.

2. On the Recipes screen, find any recipe card. REST YOUR GAZE on it without
   tapping — simply look at the card for approximately 0.6 seconds.
   ► The card will bloom: refraction depth increases by 25%, a specular
     hotspot appears and drifts toward the upper region of the card, and a
     thin luminous green rim traces the card edge. This is Apple Intelligence
     intent prediction activating the Metal 4 gaze response — the app is
     anticipating your tap before you make it.

3. Tilt the device while the card is in bloom state.
   ► The specular highlight physically tracks your tilt angle — it moves
     across the card surface like sunlight on a real glass object.

TAB MELT (repeat to verify):
4. Tap between the Recipes and Pantry tabs three times in quick succession.
   ► Each transition is a unique noise-field dissolve — because the Metal
     shader generates noise procedurally, no two melts are identical. The
     haptic pattern fires each time.

ACCESSIBILITY NOTE
If your review device has Reduce Motion enabled (Settings > Accessibility >
Motion > Reduce Motion), the refractive shaders will automatically substitute
high-contrast static mesh gradients and the Tab Bar will use a standard
cross-dissolve. This is intentional — all Metal 4 features gracefully degrade
in compliance with WCAG 2.2 AA. To see the full experience, please disable
Reduce Motion for the duration of your review.

ENERGY / BACKGROUND NOTE
All motion sensors (CMMotionManager at 30Hz) and gaze-tracking timers pause
immediately when the app enters the background (UIApplication.willResignActive).
They resume on foreground (UIApplication.didBecomeActive). No background
sensor activity occurs.
